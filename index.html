7 Ways To Avoid Walls Of Text

Narrow the column: There are various guidelines regarding the width of a text column. Depending on your font, font size, and page layout, a slightly wider or narrower column may be necessary. To determine a comfortable width, observe column widths on your favorite online media sources within context.

Use headings: Headings serve multiple purposes for website content. They provide resting points for visitors' eyes, summarize content, and aid in skimming. When skimming, visitors should be able to understand the page's content and locate relevant information by reading the headings. (Headings are also crucial for SEO keywords).

Use visuals: Incorporate photos or visualized data like graphs to break up a wall of text. Visual content communicates quickly and complements written content effectively.

Use lists: Lists simplify complex information, making it quick to comprehend. Skimming becomes effortless as information is logically organized around or in support of specific themes within the content.

Use shorter sentences and paragraphs: On the web, dense paragraphs with numerous sentences are outdated. To reduce text density, opt for paragraphs that are 2-3 sentences long.

Blockquote your quotes: Utilize blockquotes, a style inherent in most website text editors, to add quote styling. This styling breaks the monotony of continuous paragraphs.

Allow space: Ensure paragraphs and lines of text have sufficient space around them. Providing space is crucial for allowing website visitors room to breathe.
